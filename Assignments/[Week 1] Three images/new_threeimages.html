Al-Aqsa

Al-Aqsa Mosque, also known as Masjid al-Aqsa, holds a place of profound significance in the Islamic faith. Situated in the heart of the Old City of Jerusalem, it is the third holiest site in Islam, following the Kaaba in Mecca and the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. The mosque, along with the Dome of the Rock, is a symbol of spiritual and historical importance for Muslims worldwide. It is believed to be the location from which the Prophet Muhammad ascended to the heavens during the Night Journey. The mosque complex encompasses a vast area with stunning architecture and a rich history dating back over a thousand years. Al-Aqsa remains a focal point of worship, pilgrimage, and cultural heritage, as well as a symbol of the enduring connection between the Muslim community and the city of Jerusalem.

Genocide has a name

Children in Gaza, Palestine, have endured the devastating effects of the conflict, leaving many without safe and stable buildings to call home. Israel, with its genocide, is drowning every day hundreds of children's lives, losing their childhood, leaving them without homes, destroying schools, hospitals, and vital infrastructure, leaving children and their families in conditions of life insecurity. Despite these difficulties, the resilience of Gaza's children shines through as they try to rebuild their lives and dreams of a brighter future, underscoring the urgent need for international support and efforts to ensure their well-being, safety and access to education.

The Unbroken Smiles of Palestinian Children Amidst Adversity

Childhood in Gaza, despite the harsh challenges of ongoing conflict, carries within it a precious treasure of happiness and hope. These children display an extraordinary strength and joy, even as they are surrounded by the destruction and aftermath of war. They find happiness in simple moments, such as playing with friends, enjoying their favorite food, or spending a Sunday at the beach. For them, school represents an opportunity to learn and dream of a brighter future. This childhood stands as a symbol of resilience and hope for a brighter day when peace and prosperity will prevail. While the war may have a profound impact on their lives, the children of Gaza continue to beautify the world with their smiles and their desire to change and improve their society.
